Where the story begins

The Dewdrop

My first memory begins in a three-story home in Everett, Washington. I was three years old, still wearing diapers, sitting beside my younger sister at the top of the outdoor steps.

I walked down to the walkway, turned left, and noticed a blade of grass with a dewdrop at its tip. One side felt sticky and spiky. The other side was smooth. That small encounter became my first remembered lesson about individuality.

How this autobiography is built

Memory is the source.
Correction is the method.

This book is written from first-person recollection, dated records, public work, and continuing author review. It does not add invented dialogue, motives, people, or events.

01

Direct testimony

Remembered scenes remain in the author’s voice and are identified as autobiographical testimony.

02

Documented anchors

Dates, places, projects, and public milestones are connected to records where records exist.

03

No false certainty

Approximate dates and unresolved details remain visibly approximate instead of being silently completed.

04

Author control

Every chapter remains open to Steven’s additions, corrections, and final approval.
