# Steven Owens: Life, Work, and Public Record

I am **Steven Michael Allen Owens**, publicly known through **@soquarky**, **SoQuarky**, and related creative identities including **FYP.Lovely** and **Miss Lovely**.

I have spent my life building meaning from direct experience, public communication, creative technology, independent research, humor, and persistent self-education. My work does not fit neatly into one job title. I write, design, develop, research, perform, document, advocate, and turn ideas into public artifacts.

This page is a public autobiography and portfolio map. It is not a complete legal, medical, financial, or private-person record. Personal statements are identified as authored testimony. Project statements are linked to public repositories and classified by evidence state.

## What I have built

### Creative software

My longest technical project is the effort to build a free, local-first, stylus-centered drawing and frame-by-frame animation environment.

That lineage includes:

- **SoCreate** — early drawing, animation, interface, and Android experiments
- **Nexus Canvas** — an architecture-focused art-studio concept
- **InkFrame** — a Kotlin and OpenGL creative application lineage
- **InkFrame v5 / The Glass Horizon** — a browser, PWA, and Android-WebView drawing and animation studio organized around a radial glass-orb interface

Across these projects, the recurring principles are consistent: the user should own the files, the application should work offline, creative work should not require a subscription, the stylus should be treated as a primary instrument, and the interface should protect the canvas rather than dominate it.

### Independent research

I have also developed public research programs and computational proposals in several areas:

These projects occupy different evidence states. Some are formal proposals, some contain implementations or simulations, and some remain speculative. The public record uses the C0-C5 framework so that an idea, a model, a test, an independent reproduction, and real-world validation are never presented as the same thing.

## How I work

I work as a self-directed creator outside conventional institutional structures. I use consumer hardware, browser-based development, Android tooling, GitHub, AI coding agents, public writing, and repeated iteration to build an independent studio around my own ideas.

AI agents are part of the labor process, but they are not the author of my purpose. I define the direction, values, constraints, and intended public meaning. Agent output must remain inspectable, testable, correctable, and subordinate to human authorship.

## What connects the work

The projects differ, but the commitments remain stable:

1. **Ownership** — people should control their files, artwork, and tools.
2. **Access** — creative software should remain usable without expensive recurring payments.
3. **Embodied design** — tablets and styluses deserve interfaces designed around the hand, not adapted from desktop administration panels.
4. **Public accountability** — claims should be traceable to code, documents, tests, or clearly labeled testimony.
5. **Persistent authorship** — a person can build a coherent body of work even without a conventional title, employer, laboratory, or degree pathway.

## A public record, not a private-data dump

Being seen does not require publishing every private document. The public record should show the shape, labor, and meaning of a life while protecting raw medical, benefits, legal, contact, financial, location, account-export, and third-party records.

The publication repository therefore contains approved summaries and evidence-aware derivatives. The complete Life OS remains a private source archive.
