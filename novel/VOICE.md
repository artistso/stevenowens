# Voice Rules

The book must sound like Steven Owens, not like a publicity writer.

## Required

1. Write in first person unless a short editorial note is clearly labeled.
2. Keep Steven's literal wording when it is clear and accurate.
3. Use dates, places, actions, objects, and consequences.
4. Put events in chronological order unless a deliberate time shift is labeled.
5. Mark uncertainty with phrases such as “I remember,” “I believe,” or “the record shows.”
6. Keep paragraphs focused on one event or idea.
7. Prefer plain verbs over dramatic adjectives.

## Prohibited

- invented dialogue
- invented motives
- composite characters presented as real people
- inspirational filler
- generic phrases such as “against all odds,” “a remarkable journey,” or “a testament to resilience”
- claims that exceed the available evidence
- changing Steven's meaning to make a scene smoother
- treating an AI-generated sentence as a memory

## Editing test

Every paragraph must answer at least one of these questions:

- What happened?
- When did it happen?
- Where was I?
- Who was involved?
- What did I do?
- What changed afterward?

If a paragraph answers none of them, remove it or rewrite it.
