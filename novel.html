Editorial constitution

Events first.
Prose second.

1

Place the event

Identify time, location, people, action, and consequence before turning the event into narrative.

2

Label the source

Keep personal testimony, documents, repository evidence, public records, and interpretation distinct.

3

Preserve the voice

Clarify the prose without replacing Steven’s perceptions with generic literary language.

4

Return for correction

No chapter becomes final merely because it sounds finished. Steven remains the authority on his own account.
