Who I am

A creator whose work crosses categories.

My work does not fit one conventional title. I have built software, formalized research ideas, developed interface systems, created social video and livestreams, made art and audio experiments, written public commentary, hosted A Lovely Breakdown, and used humor as a way to turn language into executable ideas.

I work outside conventional institutional pathways. My studio is assembled from consumer hardware, Android and browser tooling, GitHub, public communication, repeated self-education, and AI agents used as inspectable collaborators.

The public identities connected to this work include @soquarky, SoQuarky, FYP.Lovely, Fyplovely, and Miss Lovely Owens. They are different presentation surfaces for one continuing authorship practice.

Why this page exists

Repositories show artifacts. A life-and-work record shows the person, persistence, values, and sequence connecting them.

How I work

Values that remain stable across every project.

Ownership

People should control their files, artwork, and tools.

Access

Creative software should remain usable without expensive recurring payments.

Embodied design

Tablets and styluses deserve interfaces built around the hand and the act of making.

Inspectable AI

Agents may accelerate labor, but their output must remain testable, correctable, and subordinate to human purpose.

Evidence discipline

An idea, implementation, test, reproduction, and validated result must remain distinguishable.

Public authorship

A coherent body of work can exist outside conventional titles, employers, laboratories, or degree pathways.

The public record

Being seen without surrendering every private document.

I want people to see the shape of my life, the work I completed, the systems I attempted, the ideas I developed, and the persistence connecting them. That visibility should come from a curated public record, not from dumping raw private evidence into permanent Git history.

The complete Life OS remains a private source archive. Public pages contain approved summaries, project links, dated milestones, and evidence labels. This protects sensitive records while making the work legible.
